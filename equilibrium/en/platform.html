How EQuilibrium carries an idea to a product

We work with no upfront payments and no cost to the client — on a contractual basis and within our capabilities.

EQuilibrium applies a hybrid strategy for delivering engineering projects. A “strong brain” — the Lead Designer-Conductor — runs your order as chief designer, systems engineer, chief process engineer, project director and venture partner in one, drawing in a domain brigade and independent calculation review, and carries the project through 11 gates — from a component or instrument to a machine tool, an automated line or a full production facility.

The foundation

9 iron principles

They cannot be broken. This is what separates engineering work from plausible text.

1 · Physics first

Every decision is grounded in the laws of mechanics, thermodynamics, strength of materials, mechanism theory, hydro- and aerodynamics. No “gut feeling” decisions.

2 · Units always

Every quantity carries an SI unit and a tolerance. Every calculation passes a dimensional check. Numbers without units are forbidden.

3 · No invented standards

We don’t invent standard numbers (GOST/ISO), material properties or coefficients. Anything uncertain is flagged “to be verified against the primary source”.

4 · Calculation discipline

Goal → schema and assumptions → input data → formula → substitution → result → safety factor → dimensional check → a second independent method.

5 · Traceability

Every requirement → solution → calculation → drawing element → verification item. Nothing hangs in the air.

6 · ESKD and formatting

Design docs per ESKD (or ISO/ASME): fits and tolerances, GD&T, surface finish, preferred-number series, designations.

7 · Safety and margins

Explicit safety factors, loading regimes (static/fatigue/impact/thermal cycling), worst-case and justification.

8 · Honesty about uncertainty

Where knowledge is incomplete we say so plainly, give a range and a plan to remove the uncertainty (experiment, measurement, CAE, prototype).

9 · Patent clearance

FTO analysis before any prototype spend. We don’t reproduce others’ patented solutions without analysis.

The pipeline

Stage-gate G0 → G10

Each gate is a go/no-go decision against criteria. Didn’t pass — back for rework, or stop. The next stage doesn’t open until the previous one is closed.

G0

Intake & feasibility

Brief, feasibility, technology readiness level (TRL), patent landscape, market estimate, preliminary term sheet — the share and partnership are fixed at the entry point.

G1

Concept

At least 3 fundamentally different concepts; a trade-study (Pugh matrix) on reliability / precision / manufacturability / cost / schedule / risk; selection and justification.

G2

Specifications (TS & TC)

Technical specification (per GOST), technical conditions, a matrix of measurable requirements with tolerances.

G3

Calculations, design docs, 3D

Drawing cascade (ESKD), specifications, BOM, CAE reports. Geometry — parametric, as code.

G4

Process & manufacturing

Routing and operation cards, method selection, CAM strategy, tooling, inspection operations.

G5

IP & patenting

Invention / utility-model applications, FTO analysis, protection strategy for units and solutions.

G6

Prototype & testing (V&V)

Prototype, test protocols, digital twin, requirement verification.

G7

Financing

Financial model, valuation, pitch deck, data room, raising a round for the product.

G8

Pilot / MVP production

Pilot batch, factory and site acceptance tests (FAT/SAT), cost calculation.

G9

Production & sales

Series launch, go-to-market, channels, sales contracts.

G10

Partnership & development

Formalizing the share, roadmap, innovation, version 2.0.

Quality loop and self-audit

Before every significant output — a checklist:

  • Units balance across all formulas
  • Every number has a source and a confidence marker
  • Margins are explicit, worst-case accounted for
  • Tolerance stack-up is closed
  • Resonance / thermal / fatigue risks checked
  • Every requirement traces to a test
  • FTO / patent clearance verified
