The problem

A good idea has no road to a finished product

A traditional engineering bureau is expensive, slow and takes money upfront without sharing the risk. Freelancers cover pieces but are not accountable for the result. General-purpose AI produces plausible text without engineering discipline — with invented standards and numbers that carry no units. Between “I have an idea” and “the product passes acceptance and gets sold” lies a chasm.

The solution

One Lead Designer-Conductor. The whole brigade. An engineer’s discipline.

A “strong brain” runs your order as Chief Designer and systems engineer, drawing in domain agents and independent calculation review. Every number carries a unit and a source. No invented standards.

⚙️

Engineering discipline

Physics first, units always, calculation schema → formula → substitution → safety factor → cross-check by a second method. Design docs per ESKD, GD&T, fits and tolerances.

🧭

End-to-end pipeline

Gates G0→G10: feasibility → concepts → specs → calculations and design docs → process → patent → prototype → financing → pilot → series → development. A go/no-go decision at each one.

🤝

Equity partnership

We don’t charge for “a stack of papers”. We take a share and carry the project to revenue. Our result is a product that flies, passes acceptance and sells.

📐

Geometry as code

Parametric models as scripts (CadQuery / build123d / KOMPAS API) — deterministic, verifiable geometry, not a verbal “by eye” description.

🛡️

Patent clearance

Freedom-to-operate (FTO) analysis before any prototype spend. Patenting units turns your share into a protected asset, not a napkin sketch.

🔬

Double-check

Critical calculations are run past an independent adversarial reviewer. The rule of two independent AIs reduces errors in the numbers.

The pipeline

11 gates from brief to share

The project moves through stage-gate steps. The next gate doesn’t open until the previous one is closed — which protects you from investing in a half-baked solution.

G0 · Intake & feasibility G2 · Specs (TS/TC) G3 · Calculations & design docs G5 · Patent & FTO G6 · Prototype & V&V G7 · Financing G9 · Series & sales

G0

Intake & feasibility

Brief, feasibility, TRL, patent landscape, preliminary term sheet with shares.

G1

Concept

At least 3 distinct concepts, trade-study on reliability, precision, manufacturability, cost and risk.

G3

Calculations, design docs, 3D

Drawing cascade (ESKD), specifications, BOM, CAE reports, parametric geometry.

G7

Financing

Financial model, valuation, pitch deck, data room, raising a round for the product.
